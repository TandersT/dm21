**Run ID:** wfl-2026-06-12-090330

## What this milestone delivered

The second wave of continuity work, deliberately deferred from [v1](project-continuity-graph-v1.md): the in-game timeline, contradiction detection, NPC-to-NPC knowledge propagation, and per-campaign event scoping. v1 left `TimelineTracker` dormant because freeform dates like "Dawn — first morning in Barovia" can't feed `GameTime` math — DM2-6 resolved that with a hybrid date model (prose stays the display authority, the tracker's structured clock is the math authority), and DM2-11 wired the tracker into the play loop on top of it: dedicated time tools, engine-side journal stamping, and automatic temporal-order warnings. DM2-12 and DM2-13 activated the other two dormant `claudmaster/consistency` modules as DM-facing tools — a read-only pre-narration contradiction check with persisted resolutions, and explicit rumor mechanics with confidence decay. Underneath it all, DM2-14 fixed the global `adventure_log.json` so events attribute reliably per campaign, which retroactively closes v1's known cross-campaign pollution gap in `sync_facts` and the recap.

## How the pieces fit together

**Spike → timeline wiring.** DM2-6 is a decision document, not code: it picked the hybrid date model and pinned the rules DM2-11 then implemented — the engine owns all calendar math (the LLM passes `Day N` + time of day, never arithmetic), journal writes stamp the tracker's `current_time` at write time engine-side, "unanchored" is an explicit persisted marker rather than an inferred state, anchoring must run before any stamping, and pre-anchor history gets no backfilled stamps. The wiring lives in `src/dm20_protocol/main.py` (`set_game_time`, `advance_game_time`, `get_timeline`, the `_stamp_timeline` hook in `add_event`) over the previously dormant `src/dm20_protocol/claudmaster/consistency/timeline.py`, with the tracker cached on `DnDStorage` (`src/dm20_protocol/storage.py`).

**Three siblings, one activation shape.** DM2-11, DM2-12, and DM2-13 are three instances of the same v1 pattern: take a dormant `claudmaster/consistency` module (`timeline.py`, `contradiction.py`, `npc_knowledge.py`), hold it on `DnDStorage` per campaign (loaded on campaign load/switch, cleared on close, degrading to `None` with the tools reporting "unavailable"), expose it through a small family of MCP tools with uniform campaign/availability guards, and trigger it from the persona's Continuity Protocol (`.claude/dm-persona.md`). The modules also interlock through the fact graph: timeline stamps carry `evt_*` fact ids, the contradiction detector shares the live `FactDatabase` instance so it sees facts minted mid-session by any write path, and DM2-13's reveals converge on the same `pfact_` content-derived ids as v1's `record_party_fact` — so a fact revealed to an NPC is immediately checkable by `check_consistency`.

**Scoping underneath it all.** DM2-14 moves the adventure log into the campaign directory, joining the fact graph, discovery state, timeline, and contradictions in the established per-campaign co-location pattern (`docs/STORAGE_STRUCTURE.md`). That makes the journal — the input to dual-write, `sync_facts` replays, recap assembly, and timeline stamping — campaign-scoped like everything that consumes it, so switching campaigns swaps the clock, the journal, and the stamped history together, and one campaign's backfill can no longer ingest another's events.

The cross-ticket behaviors are locked by the seal suite at `tests/test_milestone_seal_continuity_graph_v2.py`.

## Key design decisions

- **DM2-6 — hybrid date model, clock owned by `TimelineTracker`**: fully additive and backward compatible; matches the architecture's division of labor (LLM does language, engine does deterministic state) and the v1 precedents (storage-held accessor, degrade-to-None, sync_facts-style self-heal). Owning the clock on `TimelineTracker.current_time` instead of a new `GameState` field avoids a second persisted copy and its permanent drift surface. Rejected: structured-calendar-only (breaking schema/tool/prompt change, loses narrative flavor) and parse-on-write (fantasy prose has no absolute anchor; server-side NLP inverts the engine/LLM split; silent mis-parses poison temporal validation).
- **DM2-11 — dedicated time tools with explicit set/advance semantics** the prompts can name, rather than overloading `update_game_state` with 11+ structured time params; the engine owns all calendar math (honoring the spike's no-LLM-arithmetic rule); stamping at the tool layer and the `DnDStorage` lifecycle mirror the v1 fact-graph precedents. New campaigns are born anchored at epoch; legacy campaigns anchor once at resume via the persisted marker.
- **DM2-12 — in-memory pending buffer between check and resolve**: satisfies "no disk writes on check" and "disk gets exactly the DM-decided records" simultaneously; sharing the live `FactDatabase` instance is required so the detector sees facts dual-written during the session; mirrors the established tracker lifecycle and persona-activation precedents. Rejected: register-and-save-everything (first save persists every false positive ever checked) and a stateless round-trip payload (restart-proof but heavy, error-prone LLM re-entry).
- **DM2-13 — explicit DM-named propagation** matching the dormant method's shape, keeping rumor spread auditable; decay default 0.75 maps the model's 1.0-certain/0.5-rumor scale to two hops; the shared `_content_fact_id` helper prevents the dedup formula drifting between the party and NPC write paths. Rejected: automatic propagation over `NPC.relationships` (no trigger exists, name-keyed prose graph, unauditable silent world mutation) and folding into the existing interaction/party tools (muddles interaction-vs-knowledge semantics).
- **DM2-14 — per-campaign log in the campaign directory**, following the codebase's own convention for per-campaign state; the legacy global file stays as the monolithic/no-campaign fallback. Migration is conservative: unambiguous single-campaign logs auto-migrate (merge-by-id, rename-not-delete); ambiguous multi-campaign logs are left in place and excluded from views — which is itself the pollution fix.
- **Sweep — guard-pattern consolidation**: the post-run sweep found the campaign/availability guard pattern drifting across the three tool families (PRs #8–#10); it was consolidated once on the timeline branch (commit `e9100fb17`) and forward-merged through the dependent branches, so all v2 tools share one guard order and wording.

## What's NOT in this milestone

- Automatic NPC knowledge propagation over the `NPC.relationships` graph — explicitly rejected in DM2-13 (no trigger exists, the relationship graph is name-keyed prose, and silent world mutation would be unauditable). Rumor spread is always an explicit DM tool call.
- Multi-campaign legacy log migration: when more than one campaign shares a data directory, the old global `events/adventure_log.json` is left in place by design (attribution is ambiguous) — its events are excluded from all views and `sync_facts` reports the unattributed count instead of replaying them.
- The timeline is not wired into the recap: `get_session_recap` still constructs `SessionRecapGenerator(..., timeline=None)`; the recap's axis is the session number, the timeline's is the game day (the seal suite pins that they agree over the same journal).
- The simplified 12×30 calendar stays hardcoded in `GameTime`; setting-specific calendars live in the prose layer. One clock per campaign — multi-hero per-thread clocks remain external overlays.
- No retroactive `GameTime` stamps for events that predate anchoring — timeline coverage starts at the anchor point by design (no reliable source; false precision would corrupt temporal-order validation).
- No CI workflow exists yet — every PR in this run (#7–#11) has zero automated checks; tests are run locally only.

## Tests

Seal suite: `tests/test_milestone_seal_continuity_graph_v2.py` — 6 cross-ticket integration tests over real storage + campaign lifecycle (tmp-path isolation, no fakes across ticket boundaries):

- `test_each_campaign_keeps_its_own_clock_journal_and_stamped_history` — switching campaigns swaps the timeline clock, the adventure log, and the stamped history together (DM2-11 + DM2-14).
- `test_fact_backfill_replays_only_the_active_campaigns_journal` — `sync_facts` over a per-campaign log no longer ingests another campaign's events into the fact graph or recap (DM2-14 + v1 backfill), the milestone's motivating pollution bug.
- `test_resuming_a_pre_timeline_campaign_anchors_the_clock_before_stamping` — a campaign without `timeline.json` resumes unanchored, journal writes land unstamped with guidance until `set_game_time` anchors (Day 2 = epoch + 1 day), and pre-anchor history is never backfilled (DM2-6 migration rules + DM2-11).
- `test_recap_session_view_and_timeline_day_view_cover_the_same_journal` — the same journal writes appear whole on the session-number axis (v1 recap) and split correctly on the game-day axis (DM2-11).
- `test_narration_checks_catch_conflicts_with_lore_revealed_to_npcs` — lore minted through `reveal_fact_to_npc` is immediately visible to `check_consistency` via the shared live fact graph; the check persists nothing, resolving persists the ruling (DM2-12 + DM2-13).
- `test_a_played_session_survives_a_storage_reload_intact` — after clock moves, stamped writes, reveals + propagation, and a resolved contradiction, a fresh storage load restores every continuity surface from the campaign directory (DM2-11 + DM2-12 + DM2-13 + DM2-14).

Per-ticket tests live in each PR: `tests/test_timeline_wiring.py`, `tests/test_timeline_lifecycle.py`, `tests/claudmaster/test_timeline.py` (PR #8); `tests/test_contradiction_check_wiring.py`, `tests/claudmaster/test_contradiction.py` (PR #9); `tests/test_npc_knowledge_wiring.py`, `tests/claudmaster/test_npc_knowledge.py` (PR #10); `tests/test_event_campaign_scoping.py`, `tests/test_fact_dual_write.py` (PR #11).
